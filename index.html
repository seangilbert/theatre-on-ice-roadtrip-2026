Desert to the Alien Capital
Yuma, AZ · Allow 45 min
Yuma Territorial Prison State Historic Park
+
The real Old West prison that inspired "3:10 to Yuma" — original cellblocks, a guard tower, and the notorious "dark cell" used for solitary confinement, built in 1876 with two-foot-thick adobe walls. Right off I-8, about 2.5 hours into the drive from Irvine, well before Tucson. Small museum, easy walk-through, and a good first leg-stretcher of the trip.
🚛 State park lot is spacious and flat, easy access just off the interstate exit.
Tucson, AZ · Lunch Stop
Tucson — Sonoran Hot Dog
+
Tucson is America's only UNESCO City of Gastronomy and the Sonoran hot dog — bacon-wrapped, bean-topped, served in a bolillo bun — is its greatest contribution to roadside eating. Hit El Güero Canelo or BK Tacos on the east side of town, then get back on I-10. Don't leave without one.
🚛 Street parking is workable at drive-in spots; or grab a fast-food lot nearby and walk half a block.
Roswell, NM · Allow 1.5 hrs
International UFO Museum & Research Center
+
Alien autopsy dioramas, declassified government documents, witness testimonies, and a gift shop that commits fully to the bit. Roswell has been leaning into the 1947 mythology for 75 years and the result is genuinely fun. Arrive with enough daylight to walk Main Street — the whole town is in on the joke.
🚛 Park on N. Main St. municipal lot — long pull-through spaces. One person can wait with the rig while the other goes in, then swap.
Roswell, NM · Across the Street
The UFO McDonald's
+
Directly across from the UFO Museum sits the only space-and-UFO-themed McDonald's in the world — the building itself is shaped like a flying saucer. It's free, it's five minutes, and it's exactly the kind of detail that makes Roswell worth the stop. Grab a coffee and take the photo.
🚛 Same municipal lot covers both stops — no need to move the rig.
Night Stop
Roswell, NM
Hotel on Main St. or N. Pine Lodge Rd.
+
The Fairfield Inn or Holiday Inn Express on the north end of Roswell both have large open lots — straightforward trailer parking. Check in, drop the rig, and walk to dinner on Main Street. Roswell commits to the alien theme hard; lean into it.
2 Day
Roswell → Tulsa
~740 miles · US-70 E → I-40 E → I-44 E · ~11.5 hrs towing
Route 66 Country
Glenrio, TX/NM Border · Allow 30 min
Glenrio Ghost Town
+
A real ghost town straddling the Texas-New Mexico border, right off I-40 at Exit 0. Glenrio thrived on Route 66 traffic until I-40 bypassed it in the 1970s — today the State Line Motel & Cafe sign still reads "First in Texas" on one side and "Last in Texas" on the other, depending which way you're heading. An abandoned Texaco station and the Little Juarez Diner are still standing too. No admission, no gift shop, just empty buildings and wind. Watch your footing — structures are fragile.
🚛 Open gravel area right off the exit, plenty of room for a trailer. No tight access.
Amarillo, TX · Mid-Morning
Cadillac Ranch
+
Ten Cadillacs buried nose-first in a panhandle field since 1974. The paint job changes daily because everyone adds their own layer — participation is the entire point. Flat gravel frontage road, easy trailer parking. Pack two cans of Rustoleum before you leave Irvine. This stop is non-negotiable.
🚛 Park on the flat frontage road shoulder alongside other visitors. No tight turns, easy out.
Amarillo, TX · 10 Minutes South on I-27
Ozymandias on the Plains
+
Two giant legs in athletic socks stand alone in a pasture off I-27 — a deadpan homage to Shelley's poem about the ruined statue of Ramesses II. No museum, no gift shop, just enormous stone legs behind a fence with cattle wandering nearby. It's the kind of deeply weird, unphotographed-by-tour-buses stop that makes this whole trip worth doing.
🚛 Roadside pull-off, no parking lot — quick stop from the shoulder, then back on the highway.
Amarillo, TX · Lunch
The Big Texan Steak Ranch
+
The 72-ounce free steak challenge, a neon sign visible from orbit, and the spectacle of someone attempting it on a raised stage in front of strangers. You will not finish the steak. You will enjoy watching someone else try. The Big Texan has its own large lot — the most trailer-friendly lunch stop on the trip.
🚛 Large dedicated lot with room for long rigs. Easy pull-in off I-40 frontage road.
Oklahoma City, OK · Late Afternoon Pass-Through
OKC — Quick Fuel & Stretch
+
OKC sits about two-thirds of the way through today's drive — a good fuel stop and leg-stretch, but you're pushing on to Tulsa tonight rather than stopping here. If you want Cattlemen's Steakhouse in Stockyards City (open since 1910), this is the moment, but it'll cost you 30-45 minutes you don't really have. Otherwise grab fast food at the interstate exit and keep moving.
🚛 Any truck-stop fuel plaza off I-40/I-44 junction handles a trailer easily.
Night Stop
Tulsa, OK
Hotel near I-44 / Route 66 Corridor
+
Pushing 100 extra miles past OKC to Tulsa tonight is what makes tomorrow's brutal day bearable — it splits the OKC-to-Columbus haul into two reasonable days instead of one 13-hour slog. Tulsa also has real Route 66 character of its own: the 76-foot Golden Driller statue is visible from the interstate, and if you have any energy left, the Blue Whale of Catoosa is a quick detour. Chain hotels along the I-44 corridor have the surface lots you need for the trailer.

The Long One — Two Drivers Required
Springfield, IL · Optional Fuel Stop
Springfield — Lincoln's Hometown
+
Heads up: turning north for Chicago means this route runs I-44 to I-55 through central Illinois instead of I-70 through St. Louis — so the rocking chair in Cuba, MO and the Gateway Arch drive-by are off the path on this version of the trip. Springfield is a reasonable consolation stop if you want to stretch your legs: Lincoln's Tomb and his restored home are both right off the interstate, free to view from outside if you're short on time. On a day this long, even a 15-minute stretch break matters.
🚛 Truck-stop fuel plazas right off I-55 handle a trailer easily — good spot to top off before Chicago.
Chicago, IL · Quick Stop — Address TBD
Prop Drop-Off
+
This is a working stop, not a sightseeing one — drop the show props at the venue/warehouse and get back on the road. No Cloud Gate, no lakefront, no deep-dish today; there simply isn't time on a day this long. Update this card with the exact drop-off address once you have it, so the route and map can route around downtown traffic accurately rather than assuming the Loop.
🚛 If it's a quick loading-dock handoff you may be able to stay hitched. If you need to unhitch or park for a while, that adds real time to an already long day — plan for it.
Night Stop
Columbus, OH
Hotel off I-70 East Columbus
+
You'll arrive late — this is a 16-17 hour day covering Tulsa to Chicago to Columbus in one push. Two drivers trading off is not optional here; nobody should tow this distance solo. Pre-book the hotel and confirm late check-in. Chain hotels east of downtown on I-70 have the open surface lots you need for the trailer. Once you're in, you're in — the rest of the trip is comparatively easy.
4 Day
Columbus → Norwood
~640 miles · I-70 E → I-76 E → I-78 E → I-95 N · ~10 hrs towing
The Final Push East
Kutztown, PA · Allow 1 hr
Crystal Cave
+
Heads up: Roadside America (the famous miniature village once located nearby in Shartlesville) permanently closed in 2020 and its entire collection was auctioned off — it's no longer there. Crystal Cave is the real replacement: discovered in 1871, it's Pennsylvania's first show cave and still family-run. A 55-minute guided tour through limestone formations with names like the Ear of Corn and the Crystal Ballroom, plus a gift shop loaded with rocks and minerals. Right off I-78 Exit 35, open daily through late June.
🚛 Large gravel lot, easy access just off the interstate exit. No tight turns.
Columbia, NJ · Mental Reset
Delaware Water Gap
+
The Delaware River cuts through the Appalachians in a way that's genuinely beautiful. Pull into the visitor center lot — sized for tour buses, handles trailers fine. Walk to the water for ten minutes. You've driven across America in four days. Let it catch up with you before the final sprint up I-95.
🚛 Large flat visitor center lot. Fill the tank at the Columbia exit — last relaxed fuel stop before Connecticut.
Finish Line
Norwood, MA
Home
+
Roll the windows down on the Connecticut shoreline — you'll smell the salt air before you see the ocean. Four days, ~3,185 miles, one country — including a Chicago prop drop-off on the longest day of the trip. That deserves a moment.
